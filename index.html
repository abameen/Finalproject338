Detroit Traffic Injury Analysis

How roadway design relates to severe crashes on Detroit’s most dangerous streets.

Project Overview

This project examines how roadway design, lane count, and intersection complexity are connected to crash severity in Detroit.

The Problem

Detroit’s most serious crashes are concentrated on a small group of major corridors and intersections, especially along the High Injury Network.

Key Findings

  • Wider roads were linked to more severe crashes.
  • Speed limit did not show a strong relationship with severity.
  • A small number of streets and intersections accounted for many severe crashes.

Visual Highlights

The detailed charts and map used in the report support these findings and appear in the full project materials.

Recommendations

  • Prioritize the highest-risk corridors for design improvements.
  • Review signalized intersections with repeated severe crashes.
  • Use the High Injury Network to guide safety investments.
